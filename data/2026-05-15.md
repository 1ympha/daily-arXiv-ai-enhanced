### [1] [Phylogenetic Tree Inference with Tropical Axial Attention](https://arxiv.org/abs/2605.13894)
*Chris Teska,Kurt Pasque,Ruriko Yoshida,Baran Hashemi*

Main category: q-bio.PE

TL;DR: 本文提出了一种热带轴向注意力神经推理架构，用最大加算子替代标准softmax点积注意力，通过多物种序列比对学习成对距离，并利用热带格拉斯曼流形与系统发育树空间的同构关系进行系统发育推断。实验表明该方法在树度量一致性方面优于基线模型。


<details>
  <summary>Details</summary>
Motivation: 现有神经方法在系统发育推断中缺乏几何归纳偏置，难以保证输出距离矩阵满足树度量性质，尤其是在分布偏移场景下表现不佳。

Method: 提出热带轴向注意力（Tropical Axial Attention）架构，使用最大加算子替代softmax点积注意力；结合ℓ1损失、热带对称距离度量损失和超度量违反惩罚项进行训练；利用热带格拉斯曼流形与系统发育树空间的同构关系提供几何框架。

Result: 在DS1-DS11数据集上，热带模型产生的距离矩阵比基线模型更接近BME诱导的树度量，表明该方法在树度量一致性上显著优于基线。

Conclusion: 热带注意力为神经系统发育推断提供了有效的几何归纳偏置，尤其在分布偏移和需要树度量一致性的场景下具有优势。

Abstract: In this work, we introduce a Tropical Axial Attention neural reasoning architecture that replaces vanilla softmax dot-product attention with max-plus operators, inducing a piecewise-linear structure aligned with dynamic programming formulations. From multi-species sequence alignments, our model learns all possible pairwise distances and is trained using a combination of $\ell_1$ and tropical symmetric distance metric losses with an ultrametric violation penalty. We leverage the well known isomorphic relationship between the space of all phylogenetic trees with $n$ species and tropical Grassmannian to show that tropical attention provides a natural geometric framework for phylogenetic inference.
  On empirical $DS1-DS11$ alignments, where true trees are unknown, the tropical model produces distance matrices that are substantially closer to their BME-induced tree metrics than the baseline models. These results suggest that tropical attention is a useful geometric inductive bias for neural phylogenetic inference, especially under distribution shift and when tree-metric consistency is important.

### [2] [Attention-Based Multimodal Survival Prediction with Cross-Modal Bilinear Fusion](https://arxiv.org/abs/2605.13897)
*Hassan Keshvarikhojasteh,Josien P. W. Pluim,Mitko Veta*

Main category: q-bio.QM

TL;DR: 提出了一种多模态深度学习框架，整合组织病理、RNA-seq和临床数据，通过低秩双线性融合与Kaplan-Meier校准实现患者生存预测，在HR-NMIBC数据集上优于传统方法。


<details>
  <summary>Details</summary>
Motivation: 现有生存预测方法多依赖单一模态数据，缺乏有效的多模态融合策略，难以捕获不同模态间的条件交互信息，且参数效率低、可解释性差。

Method: 采用ABMIL处理病理全切片图像，前馈编码器处理RNA-seq和临床数据；通过低秩双线性跨模态融合进行成对交互建模，再经非参数Kaplan-Meier校准将风险分数映射为生存时间。

Result: 在CHIMERA挑战数据集上，该方法在预测性能上优于基于特征拼接的基线模型，并在隐藏评估队列上展现出具有竞争力的泛化能力。

Conclusion: 提出的多模态融合框架在HR-NMIBC的生存预测任务中具有良好前景，兼具参数效率与结构可解释性。

Abstract: We propose a novel multimodal deep learning framework for patient-level survival prediction, which integrates whole-slide histology features, RNA-seq expression profiles, and clinical variables. Our architecture combines an ABMIL module~\cite{ilse2018attention} for slide-level representation with feedforward encoders for RNA and clinical data. These embeddings are then integrated through low-rank bilinear cross-modal fusion~\cite{liu2018efficient} to model conditional interactions across modalities while controlling parameter growth. The model outputs continuous risk scores that are subsequently mapped to survival times using a nonparametric calibration procedure based on the Kaplan--Meier estimator~\cite{kaplan1958nonparametric}. By decomposing multimodal reasoning into independent pairwise interactions, the proposed fusion design promotes structural interpretability and parameter efficiency compared with full tensor and hierarchical fusion strategies. Experiments on the CHIMERA challenge dataset demonstrate improved predictive performance over concatenation-based baselines and competitive generalization on hidden evaluation cohorts. These results indicate that the proposed framework is a promising approach for multimodal survival prediction in HR-NMIBC. The implementation is publicly available at https://github.com/hassancpu/ChimeraChallenge2025_Task_3.

</details>
