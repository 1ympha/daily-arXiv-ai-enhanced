### [1] [Integrating opportunities and parametrized signatures for improved mutational processes estimation in extended sequence contexts](https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.21662)
*Ragnhild Laursen,Marta Pelizzola,Lasse Maretty,Asger Hobolth*

Main category: q-bio.PE

TL;DR: 本文提出了一种整合四种扩展方法的突变特征分析框架，包括引入突变机会、扩展序列上下文、使用负二项模型以及参数化特征，在扩展序列上下文场景下显著提升了突变特征估计的鲁棒性和可靠性。


<details>
  <summary>Details</summary>
Motivation: 现有突变特征分析方法在稳健性和稳定性方面存在不足，尤其是在扩展序列上下文（如两侧各2-3个侧翼核苷酸）的情况下，需要更可靠的估计方法。

Method: 将四种扩展方法整合到一个统一框架中：(a) 将突变机会纳入分析，(b) 允许扩展序列上下文，(c) 采用负二项模型替代传统泊松模型，(d) 对特征进行参数化处理。

Result: 四种扩展的组合能够产生非常稳健且可靠的突变特征；特别是在扩展序列上下文（两侧2-3个侧翼核苷酸）时，包含突变机会和参数化特征至关重要。

Conclusion: 所提出的综合框架有效提升了突变特征估计的鲁棒性与可靠性，尤其在长序列上下文场景下更具优势。

Abstract: Mutational signatures describe the pattern of mutations over the different mutation types. Each mutation type is determined by a base substitution and the flanking nucleotides to the left and right of that base substitution. Due to the widespread interest in mutational signatures, several efforts have been devoted to the development of methods for robust and stable signature estimation. Here, we combine various extensions of the standard framework to estimate mutational signatures. These extensions include (a) incorporating opportunities to the analysis, (b) allowing for extended sequence contexts, (c) using the Negative Binomial model, and (d) parametrizing the signatures. We show that the combination of these four extensions gives very robust and reliable mutational signatures. In particular, we highlight the importance of including mutational opportunities and parametrizing the signatures when the mutation types describe an extended sequence context with two or three flanking nucleotides to each side of the base substitution.

### [2] [VARIANT: Web Server for Decoding and Analyzing Viral Mutations at Genome and Protein Levels](https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.20942)
*Rui Wang,Xuhang Dai,Xin Cao,Changchuan Yin,Tamar Schlick,Guo-Wei Wei*

Main category: q-bio.QM

TL;DR: VARIANT是一个用于RNA病毒基因组突变分析的web服务器，支持多种病毒类型，能检测点突变、插入/缺失、移码等标准突变类型，还能识别被常规软件忽略的"行突变"、"热点突变"和程序性核糖体移码区域，并整合了双图拓扑分析功能。


<details>
  <summary>Details</summary>
Motivation: 现有突变分析工具在捕获复杂突变模式方面存在不足，且难以支持不同基因组架构的多种病毒家族，亟需一个全面的突变分析平台。

Method: VARIANT接受病毒参考基因组、参考蛋白序列和/或多序列比对作为输入，自动注释标准突变类型，并检测三种特殊突变模式（行突变、热点突变、PRF区域），同时集成双图拓扑分析对RNA二级结构进行分类。

Result: 支持SARS-CoV-2、HIV-1、流感H3N2、埃博拉病毒和基孔肯雅病毒等主要病毒病原体的自动分析，提供实时进度追踪、突变分布可视化和ZIP格式结果下载。

Conclusion: VARIANT为RNA病毒突变分析提供了更全面、更自动化的解决方案，填补了常规软件在检测复杂突变模式方面的空白，网址为https://variant.up.railway.app。

Abstract: A comprehensive analysis of viral mutations is essential for understanding viral evolution, disease epidemiology, diagnosis, drug resistance, etc. However, challenges remain in capturing complex mutation patterns and supporting diverse viral families with varying genome architectures. To address these needs, we present VARIANT, an web server for mutational analysis of RNA viral genomes and associated viral products across both single- and multi-segment virus genomes. The server takes as input a viral reference genome, a reference protein sequence, and/or multiple sequence alignment, and automatically provides full annotation of mutation types, including standard categories such as point mutations (missense, silent, and nonsense), insertions, deletions, or frameshift events in both coding and non-coding regions. In addition, VARIANT detects three biologically significant mutation patterns that are overlooked by conventional software/packages: ``row mutations'' (consecutive substitutions within a window of 3 nts), ``hot mutations'' (two non-consecutive substitutions within a window of 3 nts), and potential programmed ribosomal frameshifting (PRF) regions. The server currently contains automatic analysis of major viral pathogens, including SARS-CoV-2, HIV-1, Influenza H3N2, Ebola virus, and Chikungunya virus. It also allows users to analyze customized viruses. Users can track VARIANT analysis progress in real time, visualize mutation distributions, and download structured results in ZIP format. VARIANT also incorporates dual graph topology analysis to classify frameshifting element structures from dot-bracket notation input. This feature enables systematic comparison of RNA secondary structure motifs across viral families by mapping structures to a comprehensive library of dual graph topologies. The web server is freely available at https://variant.up.railway.app.

### [3] [PanGuide3D: Cohort-Robust Pancreas Tumor Segmentation via Probabilistic Pancreas Conditioning and a Transformer Bottleneck](https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.20981)
*Sunny Joy Ma,Xiang Ma*

Main category: q-bio.QM

TL;DR: 提出了一种名为PanGuide3D的跨队列鲁棒胰腺肿瘤分割架构，通过概率解剖条件（胰腺概率图）和轻量级Transformer瓶颈，在保持模型简单高效的同时，显著提升了跨队列泛化性能，尤其在小肿瘤和困难解剖位置表现优异。


<details>
  <summary>Details</summary>
Motivation: 胰腺肿瘤CT分割在临床中重要但技术挑战大：病灶小、异质性强、易与周围软组织混淆，且模型在不同数据集间（跨队列）泛化性能显著下降。现有方法复杂但未能有效解决跨队列退化问题。

Method: PanGuide3D包含一个共享3D编码器、一个预测概率胰腺图的胰腺解码器，以及一个通过可微软门控（soft gating）在多个尺度上显式条件依赖于胰腺概率的肿瘤解码器。此外，在U-Net瓶颈处添加轻量级Transformer瓶颈以捕获长程上下文。

Result: 在PanTS（队内）和MSD Task07（跨队外）测试中，PanGuide3D在总体肿瘤分割性能、跨队列泛化、小肿瘤及困难解剖区域表现均优于强基线，同时减少了不合理的假阳性。

Conclusion: 概率解剖条件化作为一种实用策略，可在端到端模型中提升跨队列鲁棒性，有望用于勾画辅助、治疗计划和多机构研究。

Abstract: Pancreatic tumor segmentation in contrast-enhanced computed tomography (CT) is clinically important yet technically challenging: lesions are often small, heterogeneous, and easily confused with surrounding soft tissue, and models that perform well on one cohort frequently degrade under cohort shift. Our goal is to improve cross-cohort generalization while keeping the model architecture simple, efficient, and practical for 3D CT segmentation. We introduce PanGuide3D, a cohort-robust architecture with a shared 3D encoder, a pancreas decoder that predicts a probabilistic pancreas map, and a tumor decoder that is explicitly conditioned on this pancreas probability at multiple scales via differentiable soft gating. To capture long-range context under distribution shift, we further add a lightweight Transformer bottleneck in the U-Net bottleneck representation. We evaluate cohort transfer by training on the PanTS (Pancreatic Tumor Segmentation) cohort and testing both in-cohort (PanTS) and out-of-cohort on MSD (Medical Segmentation Decathlon) Task07 Pancreas, using matched preprocessing and training protocols across strong baselines. We collect voxel-level segmentation metrics, patient-level tumor detection, subgroup analyses by tumor size and anatomical location, volume-conditioned performance analyses, and calibration measurements to assess reliability. Across the evaluated models, PanGuide3D achieves the best overall tumor performance and shows improved cross-cohort generalization, particularly for small tumors and challenging anatomical locations, while reducing anatomically implausible false positives. These findings support probabilistic anatomical conditioning as a practical strategy for improving cross-cohort robustness in an end-to-end model and suggest potential utility for contouring support, treatment planning, and multi-institutional studies.

</details>
