### [1] [The Curse of Black Sigatoka: A Backward Bifurcation Perspective](https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.26060)
*Bernard Asamoah Afful,Luis F. Gordillo*

Main category: q-bio.PE

TL;DR: 本文提出一个确定性病原体-宿主模型描述黑Sigatoka病（BSD）的传播动力学，发现模型存在后向分支现象（即当基本再生数R₀<1时仍存在稳定地方病平衡点），解释了单纯降低R₀可能无法控制疾病的原因。敏感性分析表明有效控制需在降低R₀之外，着重限制高风险期新生易感叶片和培育抗病品种。同时构建了随机版本模拟传播变异性。


<details>
  <summary>Details</summary>
Motivation: 黑Sigatoka病是香蕉和芭蕉种植中最具挑战性的植物病害之一，具有持久性和抗药性，常规控制策略效果有限，因此需要深入理解其传播动力学机制。

Method: 构建确定性病原体-宿主模型，考虑双传播途径（子囊孢子和分生孢子）及有性繁殖中的配偶限制；使用归一化前向敏感指数、拉丁超立方采样和偏秩相关系数进行敏感性分析；采用随机模拟算法（SSA）构建随机版本模型；利用Sobol方差基敏感性方法分析地方病平衡点。

Result: 模型出现后向分支现象，即在R₀<1时稳定地方病平衡点与无病平衡点共存。敏感性分析显示，有效控制需超越仅降低R₀的策略，应重点限制高风险期新生叶片产生和开发抗病品种。随机模拟与确定性预测进行了比较，量化了传播变异性。

Conclusion: 单纯降低基本再生数R₀至1以下不足以控制黑Sigatoka病，必须结合限制新生易感叶片和培育抗病品种等策略。非线性参数相互作用是传播变异性的主要驱动因素。

Abstract: Black Sigatoka disease (BSD), also known as black leaf streak disease, is an airborne fungal infection caused by \textit{Pseudocercospora fijiensis} that severely impacts global banana and plantain production. Its persistence and resistance to eradication make it one of the most challenging plant diseases to manage. In this paper, we propose a deterministic pathogen-host model to describe BSD dynamics. Due to dual transmission pathways (ascospores and conidia) and mate limitation in sexual reproduction, the model exhibits a backward bifurcation: a stable endemic equilibrium coexists with the disease-free equilibrium for certain parameter values in which the basic reproduction number, $\mathcal{R}_0$, is less than 1. This phenomenon explains why control strategies that solely reduce $\mathcal{R}_0$ below one may fail. For the backward bifurcation regime, we perform sensitivity analysis of the endemic equilibrium using normalized forward sensitivity indices, Latin Hypercube Sampling, and Partial Rank Correlation Coefficients. Results indicate that effective control must extend beyond $\mathcal{R}_0$ reduction and prioritize (1) limiting production of new susceptible leaves during high-risk periods and (2) developing and deploying disease-resistant plant varieties. To incorporate transmission variability, we also formulate a stochastic version of the model using the Stochastic Simulation Algorithm (SSA). Extensive numerical simulations compare stochastic realizations with deterministic predictions and quantify variability in disease dynamics. To identify the principal drivers of persistence and variability, we analyze the endemic equilibrium using Sobol's variance-based sensitivity method, which highlights the role of nonlinear parameter interactions in shaping variability.

### [2] [Rare but Resilient: Dispersal diversity buffers species vulnerability](https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.26589)
*Davide Bernardi,Giorgio Nicoletti,Prajwal Padmanabha,Samir Suweis,Sandro Azaele,Simon A. Levin,Andrea Rinaldo,Amos Maritan*

Main category: q-bio.PE

TL;DR: 本文提出了一个基于统计物理的竞争平衡框架，通过量化物种的竞争平衡指标来预测物种存续能力，该指标整合了扩散策略多样性和种间相互作用结构，可从空间丰度数据推断，无需估计物种特征或扩散参数。


<details>
  <summary>Details</summary>
Motivation: 现有预测物种存续的方法要么是参数难以估计的机制模型，要么是上下文特定且缺乏可解释性的统计工具，亟需一个兼具可解释性和数据可操作性的统一框架。

Method: 基于复杂系统统计物理，将物种生存的关键过程整合为"竞争平衡"这一可测量指标，该指标考虑了扩散策略异质性和种间相互作用结构，并可从空间丰度数据中推断。

Result: 扩散策略的异质性越大，物种在给定丰度下的脆弱性越低；在热带和温带森林数据中得到验证。

Conclusion: 该框架提供了一个系统且可解释的工具，用于评估依赖上下文的物种脆弱性，可应用于多种生态系统。

Abstract: Predicting species persistence within ecological communities is a fundamental challenge for both empirical and theoretical ecology. Existing methods span from mechanistic models, whose parameters are difficult to estimate from data, to statistical tools whose context-specific parameters are less interpretable. Here, we present a general framework, grounded in the statistical physics of complex systems, that integrates the key processes governing species survival into a single measurable quantity: the competitive balance. This metric quantifies a focal species' vulnerability beyond its abundance by incorporating the diversity of dispersal strategies and the structure of interspecific interactions within the community. Crucially, it can be inferred from spatial abundance data, thus circumventing the need to estimate species traits or dispersal parameters. Our results reveal that greater heterogeneity in dispersal strategies reduces vulnerability for a given abundance. Although we validate the framework using tropical and temperate forest data, it can be applied to a range of different ecosystems, providing a systemic and interpretable tool for assessing a context-dependent species vulnerability that accounts for its interactions with the entire community.
