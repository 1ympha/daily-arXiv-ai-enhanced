Here, we demonstrate that putative magnetoreception based on ferromagnetic particles is widespread across a diversity of bee species (72 out of 96 species tested), with no phylogenetic signal. Using illustrative examples, we demonstrate the potential of this approach for quantifying the relative merits of different coexistence narratives and for studying their synergistic effects.

All results are exact and biologically interpretable, and are supported by numerical experiments on empirical primate data.

### [4] [Pattern Formation in a Spatial Public Goods Dilemma due to Diffusive or Directed Motion](https://arxiv.org/abs/2603.21025)

TL;DR: 研究空间结构如何影响公共物品博弈中的合作演化，通过PDE模型分析扩散和趋化运动对合作模式和资源分布的影响


<details>
  <summary>Details</summary>
Motivation: 公共物品供给存在社会困境：个体有搭便车的动机，但集体需要合作生产资源。空间结构（如资源扩散和个体趋化运动）可能影响合作演化，但具体机制尚不明确

Method: 基于Young和Belmonte的模型，推导描述策略和公共物品空间分布的PDE系统，考虑个体扩散、资源扩散以及个体对资源梯度的趋化运动。通过线性稳定性分析、弱非线性分析和数值模拟研究空间模式形成

Result: 发现两种空间模式形成机制：1）高背叛者扩散率导致的图灵不稳定性；2）合作者对资源浓度梯度强敏感性导致的趋化运动不稳定性。扩散驱动的不稳定性增加全域合作和公共物品，而趋化运动降低全域合作和环境质量

The spatial structure of populations can also impact cooperation over public goods, as diffusion of public goods and intentional motion of individuals towards regions with greater resources can interact with population and public goods dynamics to produce heterogeneous patterns in the spatial distribution of strategies and resources. In this paper, we build off a model introduced by Young and Belmonte for the reaction dynamics of interacting individuals and explicit public good, deriving a system of PDEs that describes the spatial profiles of strategies and the public good in the presence of both diffusive motion of individuals and resources and chemotaxis-like directed motion of individuals in response to gradients in the concentration of public goods. Through linear stability analysis, we show that spatial patterns in strategic and public goods profiles can emerge due to either Turing instability with high defector diffusivity or a directed-motion instability through strong sensitivity of cooperators towards increasing resource concentration. We further explore the emergent spatial patterns with a mix of weakly nonlinear stability analysis and numerical simulation, showing that diffusion-driven instability appears to increase cooperation and public goods across the spatial domain, while directed motion of cooperators towards regions with great public goods provision tends to decrease cooperation and environmental quality across the environment.

  <summary>Details</summary>

Result: 根据综合评估指标，CMPV方法在所有时间间隔上的平均得分为0.33，优于CRBC的0.48分，证明了所提插补方法在准确填充缺失心率值方面的有效性。

Moreover, this study facilitates the detection of abnormal physiological signals, enabling the implementation of early preventive measures for more accurate diagnosis.

### [6] [CERN: Correcting Errors in Raw Nanopore Signals Using Hidden Markov Models](https://arxiv.org/abs/2603.20420)
*Simon Ambrozak,Ulysse McConnell,Bhargav Srinivasan,Burak Ozkan,Can Firtina*

Main category: q-bio.GN

TL;DR: CERN是一个用于检测和校正纳米孔测序原始信号错误的机制，通过训练隐马尔可夫模型来提高原始信号分析的准确性，无需进行碱基识别。


<details>
  <summary>Details</summary>
Motivation: 纳米孔测序能够读取比传统方法更长的核酸序列，但原始电信号常因噪声和处理错误而包含误差，限制了原始信号分析的准确性。现有方法虽然可以在不进行碱基识别的情况下分析原始信号，但误差问题仍未解决。

Method: 提出CERN机制，训练并利用隐马尔可夫模型（HMM）来准确校正信号错误。该方法能够系统性地识别和校正纳米孔原始信号中的错误，为后续分析提供更准确的数据。

Result: 在多种数据集（包括大肠杆菌、果蝇和人类基因组）上的广泛评估表明：1）CERN持续提高了底层原始信号分析工具的映射准确性；2）最小化了新纳米孔化学技术下分割算法优化的负担；3）运行时不造成显著的计算开销。

Conclusion: CERN提供了一种有效的机制，能够在进一步分析之前系统性地识别和校正纳米孔原始信号中的错误，这有助于开发专门针对纳米孔原始信号的新型误差校正机制。

Abstract: Nanopore sequencing can read substantially longer sequences of nucleic acid molecules than other sequencing methods, which has led to advances in genomic analysis such as the gapless human genome assembly. By analyzing the raw electrical signal reads that nanopore sequencing generates from molecules, existing works can map these reads without translating them into DNA characters (i.e., basecalling), allowing for quick and efficient analysis of sequencing data. However, raw signals often contain errors due to noise and mistakes when processing them, which limits the overall accuracy of raw signal analysis.
  Our goal in this work is to detect and correct errors in raw signals to improve the accuracy of raw signal analyses. To this end, we propose CERN, a mechanism that trains and utilizes a Hidden Markov Model (HMM) to accurately correct signal errors. Our extensive evaluation on various datasets including E. coli, Fruit Fly, and Human genomes shows that CERN 1) consistently improves the overall mapping accuracy of the underlying raw signal analysis tools, 2) minimizes the burden on segmentation algorithm optimization with newer nanopore chemistries, and 3) functions without causing substantial computational overhead. We conclude that CERN provides an effective mechanism to systematically identify and correct the errors in raw nanopore signals before further analysis, which can enable the development of a new class of error correction mechanisms purely designed for raw nanopore signals.
  CERN is available at https://github.com/STORMgroup/CERN. We also provide the scripts to fully reproduce our results on our GitHub page.

</details>
