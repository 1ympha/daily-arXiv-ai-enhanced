### [1] [Adaptive dynamics of eco-evolutionary repeated games: Effect of reward and punishment](https://arxiv.org/abs/2601.16593)
*Prosanta Mandal,Suman Chakraborty,Vaibhav Madhok,Sagar Chakraborty*

Main category: q-bio.PE

TL;DR: 该研究开发了一个生态演化框架，分析直接互惠策略与可再生公共资源之间的耦合动态，探讨制度激励（奖励和惩罚）如何防止公地悲剧，并发现资源状态和种群策略可能出现稳定振荡。


<details>
  <summary>Details</summary>
Motivation: 研究长期演化过程如何通过产生新性状或行为来影响公共资源保护，这些新特征会改变种群策略与资源状态之间的反馈关系。

Method: 开发了一个生态演化框架，个体与相同对手重复互动，通过反应策略遵循直接互惠。将战略动态与可再生公共资源耦合，使用自适应动力学进行分析。对2×2战略博弈进行详尽的非线性动力学分析。

Result: 分析了制度激励（奖励和惩罚）在防止公地悲剧中的比较和联合效用，即使当资源充足状态下背叛占主导时。发现了通过霍普夫分岔出现的资源状态和种群策略的稳健稳定振荡。

Conclusion: 长期演化过程与制度激励相结合可以有效管理公共资源，防止公地悲剧，并且系统可能出现复杂的动态行为，包括稳定振荡。

Abstract: Long-term evolutionary processes can strongly influence common-pool resource conservation by generating new traits or behaviours that modify the feedback between population strategies and the resource state. Here we develop an eco-evolutionary framework in which individuals repeatedly interact with the same opponent and follow direct reciprocity through reactive strategies. The strategic dynamics is coupled to a renewable common resource and analyzed using adaptive dynamics. After our exhaustive non-linear dynamical analysis of $2\times2$ strategic games, we focus on comparative and combined usefulness of institutional incentives in the form of rewards and punishments in preventing the Tragedy of the Commons even when defection dominates in the replete resource state. We also report possibility of robust stable oscillations -- emerging via Hopf bifurcation -- in resource state and population strategies.
