### [1] [PhageMind: Generalized Strain-level Phage Host Range Prediction via Meta-learning](https://arxiv.org/abs/2601.15886)
*Yang Shen,Keming Shi,Chen Yu,Rui Zhang,Yanni Sun,Jiayu Shang*

Main category: q-bio.GN

TL;DR: PhageMind是一个能够跨细菌属高效迁移知识的深度学习框架，用于准确预测菌株水平的噬菌体-宿主相互作用，特别是在数据有限的情况下。


<details>
  <summary>Details</summary>
Motivation: 现有计算方法存在重大局限性：许多方法依赖于属特异性特征，无法跨分类群泛化；其他方法需要大量训练数据，而大多数细菌谱系缺乏这些数据。这迫切需要能够准确预测跨不同细菌属的菌株水平噬菌体-宿主相互作用的方法，特别是在数据有限条件下。

Method: PhageMind是一个学习框架，通过从研究充分的系统中识别噬菌体-细菌相互作用的共享原理，并利用少量已知相互作用快速将这些原理适应到新属。该方法使用知识图谱表示噬菌体-宿主关系，明确纳入噬菌体尾纤维蛋白和细菌O-抗原生物合成基因簇，并用这种表示指导相互作用预测。

Result: 在四个细菌属（大肠杆菌、克雷伯菌、弧菌和交替单胞菌）上，PhageMind实现了高预测准确性，并显示出对新谱系的强适应性。特别是在留一属评估中，当只有有限参考数据可用时，模型保持稳健性能。

Conclusion: PhageMind展示了作为研究全球噬菌体组中噬菌体-宿主相互作用的可扩展实用工具的潜力，能够准确预测跨细菌属的菌株水平相互作用，特别是在数据有限条件下。

Abstract: Bacteriophages (phages) are key regulators of bacterial populations and hold great promise for applications such as phage therapy, biocontrol, and industrial fermentation. The success of these applications depends on accurately determining phage host range, which is often specific at the strain level rather than the species level. However, existing computational approaches face major limitations: many rely on genus-specific features that do not generalize across taxa, while others require large amounts of training data that are unavailable for most bacterial lineages. These challenges create a critical need for methods that can accurately predict strain-level phage-host interactions across diverse bacterial genera, particularly under data-limited conditions. We present PhageMind, a learning framework designed to address this challenge by enabling efficient transfer of knowledge across bacterial genera. PhageMind is trained to identify shared principles of phage-bacterium interactions from well-studied systems and to rapidly adapt these principles to new genera using only a small number of known interactions. To reflect the biological basis of infection, we represent phage-host relationships using a knowledge graph that explicitly incorporates phage tail fiber proteins and bacterial O-antigen biosynthesis gene clusters, and we use this representation to guide interaction prediction. Across four bacterial genera (Escherichia, Klebsiella, Vibrio, and Alteromonas), PhageMind achieves high prediction accuracy and shows strong adaptability to new lineages. In particular, in leave-one-genus-out evaluations, the model maintains robust performance when only limited reference data are available, demonstrating its potential as a scalable and practical tool for studying phage-host interactions across the global phageome.

### [2] [ECGomics: An Open Platform for AI-ECG Digital Biomarker Discovery](https://arxiv.org/abs/2601.15326)
*Deyun Zhang,Jun Li,Shijia Geng,Yue Wang,Shijie Chen,Sumei Fan,Qinghao Zha,Shenda Hong*

Main category: q-bio.QM

TL;DR: ECGomics是一个将心电图信号系统解构为数字生物标志物的开源平台，结合专家定义的形态学规则和数据驱动的潜在表示，弥合了传统特征工程与深度学习之间的差距。


<details>
  <summary>Details</summary>

Method: 受基因组学分类严谨性的启发，ECGomics从四个维度解构心脏活动：结构、强度、功能和比较维度。该分类法将专家定义的形态学规则与数据驱动的潜在表示相结合，创建了一个可扩展的生态系统，包括基于Web的研究平台和移动集成解决方案。

Result: 开发了包含Web平台和移动界面的可部署软件生态系统。Web平台支持高通量分析、精确参数配置、高保真数据摄取和12导联可视化；移动界面集成便携传感器和云端引擎，实现实时信号采集和即时结构化诊断报告生成。

Conclusion: ECGomics协调了诊断精度、可解释性和数据效率，为数字生物标志物发现和个性化心血管医学建立了坚实基础，实现了从理论发现到分散式现实世界健康管理的转变。

Abstract: Background: Conventional electrocardiogram (ECG) analysis faces a persistent dichotomy: expert-driven features ensure interpretability but lack sensitivity to latent patterns, while deep learning offers high accuracy but functions as a black box with high data dependency. We introduce ECGomics, a systematic paradigm and open-source platform for the multidimensional deconstruction of cardiac signals into digital biomarker. Methods: Inspired by the taxonomic rigor of genomics, ECGomics deconstructs cardiac activity across four dimensions: Structural, Intensity, Functional, and Comparative. This taxonomy synergizes expert-defined morphological rules with data-driven latent representations, effectively bridging the gap between handcrafted features and deep learning embeddings. Results: We operationalized this framework into a scalable ecosystem consisting of a web-based research platform and a mobile-integrated solution (https://github.com/PKUDigitalHealth/ECGomics). The web platform facilitates high-throughput analysis via precision parameter configuration, high-fidelity data ingestion, and 12-lead visualization, allowing for the systematic extraction of biomarkers across the four ECGomics dimensions. Complementarily, the mobile interface, integrated with portable sensors and a cloud-based engine, enables real-time signal acquisition and near-instantaneous delivery of structured diagnostic reports. This dual-interface architecture successfully transitions ECGomics from theoretical discovery to decentralized, real-world health management, ensuring professional-grade monitoring in diverse clinical and home-based settings. Conclusion: ECGomics harmonizes diagnostic precision, interpretability, and data efficiency. By providing a deployable software ecosystem, this paradigm establishes a robust foundation for digital biomarker discovery and personalized cardiovascular medicine.

### [3] [Flocking by stopping: a novel mechanism of emergent order in collective movement](https://arxiv.org/abs/2601.15362)
*Yogesh Kumar KC,Arshed Nabeel,Srikanth Iyer,Vishwesha Guttal*

Main category: q-bio.QM

TL;DR: 论文提出了一种新的集体运动模型，个体通过"停止交互"机制（遇到相反方向运动的邻居时停止）而非传统的方向平均，能在大型群体中产生持续的有序运动。


<details>
  <summary>Details</summary>
Motivation: 传统集体运动模型依赖个体对多个邻居方向取平均来产生有序运动，或在小型群体中通过噪声诱导有序。本文受真实动物运动启发，探索通过停止状态和停止交互产生集体有序的新机制。

Method: 提出新模型：个体可在两个方向运动或保持静止；引入"停止交互"机制——当个体遇到相反方向运动的邻居时可能停止而非立即对齐；使用确定性和随机平均场近似分析条件；通过基于个体的模拟验证理论预测。

Result: 研究表明，通过停止交互机制，即使在大规模群体中也能产生持续的集体有序运动。这种"通过停止的集群"行为代表了一种与传统平均机制或噪声诱导有序根本不同的新机制。

Conclusion: 引入停止状态和停止交互为集体运动中的有序形成提供了新途径，揭示了不同于传统平均机制的集体有序产生机制，对理解真实动物集体运动有重要意义。

Abstract: Collective movement is observed widely in nature, where individuals interact locally to produce globally ordered, coherent motion. In typical models of collective motion, each individual takes the average direction of multiple neighbors, resulting in ordered movement. In small flocks, noise induced order can also emerge with individuals copying only a randomly chosen single neighbor at a time. We propose a new model of collective movement, inspired by how real animals move, where individuals can move in two directions or remain stationary. We demonstrate that when individuals interact with a single neighbor through a novel form of halting interaction -- where an individual may stop upon encountering an oppositely moving neighbor rather than instantly aligning -- persistent collective order can emerge even in large populations. This represents a fundamentally different mechanism from conventional averaging-based or noise-induced ordering. Using deterministic and stochastic mean-field approximations, we characterize the conditions under which such ``flocking by stopping'' behavior can occur, and confirm the mean-field predictions using individual-based simulations. Our results highlight how incorporating a stopped state and halting interactions can generate new routes to order in collective movement.

### [4] [Final size of a structured SIRD Model with active-population force of infection](https://arxiv.org/abs/2601.15388)
*Alison M. V. D. L. Melo,Matheus C. Santos*

Main category: q-bio.PE

TL;DR: 本文研究了一个具有非对称交互的两群体SIRD流行病模型，其中感染力取决于每个群体的活跃（存活）人口而非总人口，与传统模型不同。证明了易感者的最终状态总是正的，并将其表征为一个映射的唯一不动点。将最终规模与基本再生数联系起来，并显示当传播率增加时最终易感者数量减少。数值模拟比较了活跃人口模型和传统两群体SIRD模型，展示了最终规模和多次疫情波发生的差异。


<details>
  <summary>Details</summary>
Motivation: 传统SIRD流行病模型假设感染力取决于总人口，但在现实世界中，感染传播实际上依赖于活跃的存活人口。特别是在具有非对称交互的两群体系统中，这种差异可能显著影响疫情动态和最终规模预测。因此需要研究基于活跃人口的模型来更准确地描述流行病传播。

Method: 提出了一个改进的两群体SIRD流行病模型，其中感染力取决于每个群体的活跃（存活）人口而非总人口。使用数学分析证明了易感者最终状态的正性，并将其表征为一个映射的唯一不动点。建立了最终规模与基本再生数之间的关系。通过数值模拟比较了活跃人口模型与传统两群体SIRD模型的差异。

Result: 证明了易感者的最终状态总是正的，并找到了其作为映射唯一不动点的数学表征。建立了最终规模与基本再生数的明确关系，显示传播率增加会导致最终易感者数量减少。数值模拟显示活跃人口模型与传统模型在最终规模和疫情波动态方面存在显著差异，活跃人口模型可能预测不同的最终规模和多次疫情波的发生。

Conclusion: 基于活跃人口的SIRD模型提供了更现实的流行病传播描述，特别是在非对称交互的两群体系统中。该模型能够更准确地预测最终规模和疫情动态，包括多次疫情波的可能性。研究结果为流行病建模提供了新的理论框架，强调了考虑活跃人口而非总人口的重要性。

Abstract: We consider a SIRD epidemic model for a population composed of two groups of individuals with asymmetric interactions, where the force of infection depends on the active (alive) population in each group, rather than on the total population, as in the classical formulation. We prove that the final state for susceptible individuals is always positive and characterize it as the unique fixed point of a map. We also relate the final size to the basic reproduction number and show that the final number of susceptibles decreases when transmission rates increase. Numerical simulations compare the active-population and classical two-group SIRD models, showing differences in final size and the occurrence of multiple epidemic waves. The convergence of the fixed point approach is also illustrated.

### [5] [A computation of maximum likelihood for 4-states-triplets under Jukes-Cantor and MC](https://arxiv.org/abs/2601.15517)
*Maria Emilíia Alonso,Ernesto Álvarez*

Main category: q-bio.PE

TL;DR: 该论文证明了在特定条件下，三叶系统发育树的Jukes-Cantor模型似然函数具有唯一解析最大值


<details>
  <summary>Details</summary>
Motivation: 研究ChorHendySnir2006进化模型，验证其关于三叶系统发育树在Jukes-Cantor模型和分子钟假设下似然函数具有唯一解析最大值的猜想

Method: 使用微分拓扑方法将证明简化为代数问题，然后利用Maple数学软件库进行计算求解

Result: 在参数满足特定不等式条件下，证明了似然函数存在唯一的最大值，且该最大值解析依赖于参数

Conclusion: 证实了ChorHendySnir2006的猜想，为三叶系统发育树的Jukes-Cantor模型提供了严格的数学证明

Abstract: We study the ChorHendySnir2006 evolutionary model, which consists of a rooted phylogenetic tree with three leaves, subject to the Jukes--Cantor (JC69) molecular evolutionary model and molecular clock. We show that the likelihood function associated with this model has a unique maximum which depends analytically of the parameters (as it was conjectured in ChorHendySnir2006), assuming that these parameters verify some very precise inequalities; some of which arise naturally from the model. With a typical argument of differential topology we reduce the proof to answer a question of algebra, very simple, although computationally involved, that we solve using some Maple libraries. We are very indebted to Marta Casanellas, who presented the problem to us and gave us the first insights on it.
