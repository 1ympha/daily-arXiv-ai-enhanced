### [1] [Bounds on $R_0$ and final epidemic size when the next-generation matrix $M$ is only partially known](https://arxiv.org/abs/2602.23885)
*Andrea Bizzotto,Frank Ball,Tom Britton*

Main category: q-bio.PE

TL;DR: 研究多类型SIR传染病模型，在仅知道下一代矩阵M的行和或列和的部分信息情况下，推导基本再生数R0和最终流行规模τi的上下界。


<details>
  <summary>Details</summary>
Motivation: 在实际传染病建模中，通常无法完全获知不同类型个体间的接触矩阵M，而只能获得部分信息（如行和或列和）。需要研究在这种部分信息情况下，如何估计关键流行病学参数R0和最终流行规模。

Method: 建立多类型SIR模型，考虑下一代矩阵M仅通过行和{ri}或列和{cj}部分已知的情况。分析两种情形：一般矩阵M和满足细致平衡条件的接触矩阵（常见情形）。使用数学推导方法获得R0和τi的上下界。

Result: 对于一般矩阵M，获得了R0和τi的尖锐上下界。对于满足细致平衡条件的矩阵，R0的界比一般情况更窄但仍非尖锐，而最终规模的界仅在两种类型个体情况下获得。

Conclusion: 在部分信息情况下，可以推导出关键流行病学参数的界限。对于满足细致平衡条件的常见情形，问题更为复杂，需要进一步研究以获得尖锐界限。

Abstract: We study a multitype SIR epidemic model where individuals are categorized into different types, and where infection spread is characterized by a next-generation matrix $M=\{m_{ij}\}$ with community fractions $\{π_j\}$ for the different types of individuals. We analyse two key quantities: the basic reproduction number $R_0$ and the final epidemic outcome of the different types $\{τ_i\}$. We consider the situation where $M$ is only partly known, through the row sums $\{r_i\}$ or the column sums $\{c_j\}$, and treat both a general $M$ and the special but common situation where $M$ is proportional to a contact matrix satisfying detailed balance. For a general $M$, which is partially observed through $\{r_i\}$ or $\{c_j\}$, we obtain sharp upper and lower bounds of $R_0$ and $\{τ_i\}$, but for the case where $M$ satisfies detailed balance the problem is harder: our obtained bounds for $R_0$ are narrower than the general case but still not sharp, and bounds for the final size are only obtained when there are two types of individual.

### [2] [The interplay of selection and dormancy in a Moran model can lead to coexistence of types](https://arxiv.org/abs/2602.24106)
*Jochen Blath,Baptiste Le Duigou,András Tóbiás*

Main category: q-bio.PE

TL;DR: 本文提出一个包含休眠策略的Moran模型，研究具有生殖优势的居民种群与具有休眠策略的入侵种群之间的演化动力学，发现休眠策略不仅能入侵并固定，还能在特定条件下实现稳定的共存状态。


<details>
  <summary>Details</summary>
Motivation: 研究两种不同演化策略的相互作用：第一种类型在生殖过程中具有选择优势，第二种类型通过暂时进入休眠状态来避免在生殖过程中被替换。探索休眠策略如何入侵具有选择优势的居民种群，以及两种策略之间的动态平衡。

Method: 提出一个Moran模型来描述两种类型的种群动态。使用分支过程比较、动力系统近似以及渐近行为分析等方法进行研究。在系统的大种群极限下分析入侵动力学，特别关注Moran模型的固定总体大小特性以及休眠状态的死亡率和复苏率。

Result: 研究发现休眠策略不仅能在适当参数假设下入侵并随后固定（尽管存在选择劣势），而且还存在一种新的稳定共存机制。这种共存源于频率依赖的平衡效应，这种效应在先前具有Lotka-Volterra型对称竞争的设置中没有出现。共存机制的出现部分依赖于Moran建模框架的特定属性，特别是其固定的总体大小强制死亡事件后立即重新定殖的特性，以及休眠状态的（正）死亡率和复苏率。

Conclusion: 休眠策略作为一种演化策略，不仅能够克服选择劣势成功入侵和固定，还能在Moran模型的特定框架下实现与选择优势类型的稳定共存。这种共存机制源于频率依赖的平衡效应和模型的结构特性，为理解不同演化策略的相互作用提供了新的理论视角。

Abstract: In this paper we propose a Moran model that describes the population dynamics of two types: While the first type has a selective advantage during reproduction, the second type can avoid replacement during reproduction with some positive probability by switching temporarily into a dormant state. We investigate the interplay of both evolutionary strategies by studying the invasion dynamics of the dormant type into the resident (selectively advantageous) population in the large population limit of the system. It turns out that the dormancy trait can not only invade and subsequently fixate under suitable parameter assumptions despite its selective disadvantage (a phenomenon that has already been observed in a related context in Blath and Tóbiás (2020)), but that there is also a novel regime of stable coexistence of both types due to a frequency-dependent balancing effect that did not arise in the previous setup with Lotka--Volterra type symmetric competition. The emergence of a coexistence regime here rests in part on specific properties of the Moran modelling framework, in particular its fixed overall population size that enforces instant re-colonization after death events, as well as on the (positive) mortality and resuscitation rates of the dormant state. We provide heuristic explanations for the observed types of behaviour and the corresponding proofs, which involve comparisons to suitable branching processes, approximations by dynamical systems, and an analysis of asymptotic behaviour of the latter.

</details>
