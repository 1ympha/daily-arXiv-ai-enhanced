### [1] [PREFER: An Ontology for the PREcision FERmentation Community](https://arxiv.org/abs/2602.16755)
*Txell Amigó,Shawn Zheng Kai Tan,Angel Luu Phanthanourak,Sebastian Schulz,Pasquale D. Colaianni,Dominik M. Maszczyk,Ester Milesi,Ivan Schlembach,Mykhaylo Semenov Petrov,Marta Reventós Montané,Lars K. Nielsen,Jochen Förster,Bernhard Ø. Palsson,Suresh Sudarsan,Alberto Santos*

Main category: q-bio.OT

TL;DR: PREFER是一个开源本体，旨在为生物过程数据建立统一标准，解决精准发酵中数据互操作性问题，支持机器学习模型训练。


<details>
  <summary>Details</summary>
Motivation: 精准发酵依赖微生物细胞工厂生产可持续产品，高通量生物反应器平台产生大量数据，但缺乏社区标准限制了数据的可访问性和互操作性，阻碍跨平台集成。

Method: 引入PREFER开源本体，基于广泛采用的基本形式本体（BFO）构建，并与多个社区本体连接，覆盖整个精准发酵过程，确保一致性和跨域兼容性。

Result: PREFER标准化能够连接不同的数据孤岛，生成机器可操作的数据集，为训练预测性、稳健的机器学习模型提供关键支持，奠定可扩展、互操作的生物过程系统基础。

Conclusion: PREFER为精准发酵数据提供了统一标准，支持结构化元数据和自动化跨平台执行，推动生物生产向更数据驱动的方向转型。

Abstract: Precision fermentation relies on microbial cell factories to produce sustainable food, pharmaceuticals, chemicals, and biofuels. Specialized laboratories such as biofoundries are advancing these processes using high-throughput bioreactor platforms, which generate vast datasets. However, the lack of community standards limits data accessibility and interoperability, preventing integration across platforms. In order to address this, we introduce PREFER, an open-source ontology designed to establish a unified standard for bioprocess data. Built in alignment with the widely adopted Basic Formal Ontology (BFO) and connecting with several other community ontologies, PREFER ensures consistency and cross-domain compatibility and covers the whole precision fermentation process. Integrating PREFER into high-throughput bioprocess development workflows enables structured metadata that supports automated cross-platform execution and high-fidelity data capture. Furthermore, PREFER's standardization has the potential to bridge disparate data silos, generating machine-actionable datasets critical for training predictive, robust machine learning models in synthetic biology. This work provides the foundation for scalable, interoperable bioprocess systems and supports the transition toward more data-driven bioproduction.

### [2] [Exploring the Utility of MALDI-TOF Mass Spectrometry and Antimicrobial Resistance in Hospital Outbreak Detection](https://arxiv.org/abs/2602.16737)
*Chang Liu,Jieshi Chen,Alexander J. Sundermann,Kathleen Shutt,Marissa P. Griffith,Lora Lee Pless,Lee H. Harrison,Artur W. Dubrawski*

Main category: q-bio.QM

TL;DR: 该研究探索使用MALDI-TOF质谱和抗菌素耐药性模式作为全基因组测序的快速、经济替代方案，通过机器学习框架进行医院感染暴发检测


<details>
  <summary>Details</summary>
Motivation: 全基因组测序虽然是医院感染暴发检测的金标准，但成本高、耗时长，难以在临床实验室常规实施。需要寻找快速、经济的替代方法。

Method: 开发机器学习框架，从MALDI-TOF质谱和抗菌素耐药性模式中提取信息表示，用于暴发检测，并探索两者的融合方法。

Result: 通过多物种分析表明，在某些情况下，MALDI-TOF和抗菌素耐药性模式有潜力减少对全基因组测序的依赖，实现更易获得和快速的暴发监测。

Conclusion: MALDI-TOF质谱和抗菌素耐药性模式可作为全基因组测序的可行替代方案，通过机器学习方法能够实现快速、经济的医院感染暴发检测。

Abstract: Accurate and timely identification of hospital outbreak clusters is crucial for preventing the spread of infections that have epidemic potential. While assessing pathogen similarity through whole genome sequencing (WGS) is considered the gold standard for outbreak detection, its high cost and lengthy turnaround time preclude routine implementation in clinical laboratories. We explore the utility of two rapid and cost-effective alternatives to WGS, matrix-assisted laser desorption ionization-time of flight (MALDI-TOF) mass spectrometry and antimicrobial resistance (AR) patterns. We develop a machine learning framework that extracts informative representations from MALDI-TOF spectra and AR patterns for outbreak detection and explore their fusion. Through multi-species analyses, we demonstrate that in some cases MALDI-TOF and AR have the potential to reduce reliance on WGS, enabling more accessible and rapid outbreak surveillance.

### [3] [FUNGAR: a pipeline for detecting antifungal resistance mutations directly from metagenomic short reads](https://arxiv.org/abs/2602.16728)
*Henrique RM Antoniolli,Lívia Kmetzsch,Charley C Staats*

Main category: q-bio.GN

TL;DR: FUNGAR是一个开源流程，用于直接从短读长数据中快速鉴定抗真菌耐药基因和突变，特别针对宏基因组样本，解决了真菌病原体检测工具缺乏的问题。


<details>
  <summary>Details</summary>
Motivation: 抗真菌耐药性已成为临床和环境健康中日益严重的全球性问题。直接从测序reads（特别是宏基因组样本）中检测已知的耐药突变仍然是一个主要挑战。与细菌病原体相比，真菌病原体常被忽视，现有工具大多针对细菌分类群，而针对真菌的工具通常需要组装基因组。在宏基因组数据集中，基于组装的策略可能因基因组碎片化、低丰度物种或耐药位点恢复不完全而导致大量信息丢失。

Method: FUNGAR采用DIAMOND进行翻译比对，并利用FungAMR数据库的精选数据，在所有六个开放阅读框中检测氨基酸替换。该流程生成结构化、可重复的报告，将检测到的变异与相关抗真菌药物联系起来，并可轻松定制以适应新物种或数据库。

Result: 开发了FUNGAR开源流程，能够直接从短读长数据中快速识别抗真菌耐药基因和突变，特别适用于宏基因组样本分析。

Conclusion: FUNGAR填补了真菌耐药性检测工具的空缺，为直接从测序reads中检测抗真菌耐药突变提供了有效的解决方案，特别适用于宏基因组分析场景。

Abstract: Motivation: Antifungal resistance has become an increasing global concern in both clinical and environmental health. Detecting known resistance mutations directly from sequencing reads, in special metagenomic samples, remains a major challenge. As fungal pathogens are often neglected compared with bacterial pathogens, most available tools are designed for bacterial taxa, whereas tools targeting fungi typically require assembled genomes. In metagenomic datasets, assembly-based strategies may result in substantial information loss due to genome fragmentation, low-abundance species, or incomplete recovery of resistance loci. Results: Here, we present FUNGAR, an open-source pipeline for the rapid identification of antifungal resistance genes and mutations directly from short-read data. FUNGAR employs translated alignments with DIAMOND and curated data from the FungAMR database to detect amino acid substitutions across all six open reading frames. The pipeline produces structured, reproducible reports linking detected variants to their associated antifungal drugs and can be easily customized for new species or databases.

### [4] [Systematic Evaluation of Single-Cell Foundation Model Interpretability Reveals Attention Captures Co-Expression Rather Than Unique Regulatory Signal](https://arxiv.org/abs/2602.17532)
*Ihor Kendiukhov*

Main category: q-bio.GN

TL;DR: 该研究提出了一个系统评估框架（37项分析、153项统计检验、4种细胞类型、2种扰动模式）来评估单细胞基础模型的机制可解释性。应用于scGPT和Geneformer发现：注意力模式编码了层特异性生物信息，但对扰动预测无增量价值；基因级基线优于注意力边；CSSI方法将GRN恢复提升1.85倍。


<details>
  <summary>Details</summary>
Motivation: 当前单细胞基础模型的机制可解释性缺乏系统评估标准，需要建立可重复的质量控制标准来评估注意力机制是否真正编码了生物学机制信息。

Method: 开发了包含37项分析、153项统计检验的系统评估框架，应用于scGPT和Geneformer模型，分析4种细胞类型和2种扰动模式。使用注意力模式分析、相关性边分析、因果消融等方法，并提出了细胞状态分层可解释性（CSSI）方法。

Result: 注意力模式确实编码了结构化生物信息（早期层：蛋白质相互作用；晚期层：转录调控），但对扰动预测无增量价值（AUROC 0.81-0.88 vs 0.70）。基因级基线始终优于注意力边，成对边得分无预测贡献，调控头因果消融无性能下降。CSSI方法将GRN恢复提升达1.85倍。

Conclusion: 单细胞基础模型的注意力机制编码了生物学信息但无预测价值，需要新的可解释性方法如CSSI。该框架为领域建立了可重复的质量控制标准，揭示了当前注意力机制在机制可解释性方面的局限性。

Abstract: We present a systematic evaluation framework - thirty-seven analyses, 153 statistical tests, four cell types, two perturbation modalities - for assessing mechanistic interpretability in single-cell foundation models. Applying this framework to scGPT and Geneformer, we find that attention patterns encode structured biological information with layer-specific organisation - protein-protein interactions in early layers, transcriptional regulation in late layers - but this structure provides no incremental value for perturbation prediction: trivial gene-level baselines outperform both attention and correlation edges (AUROC 0.81-0.88 versus 0.70), pairwise edge scores add zero predictive contribution, and causal ablation of regulatory heads produces no degradation. These findings generalise from K562 to RPE1 cells; the attention-correlation relationship is context-dependent, but gene-level dominance is universal. Cell-State Stratified Interpretability (CSSI) addresses an attention-specific scaling failure, improving GRN recovery up to 1.85x. The framework establishes reusable quality-control standards for the field.
