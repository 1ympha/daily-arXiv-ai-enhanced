### [1] [Meeting times on graphs in near-cubic time](https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.18872)
*Alex McAvoy*

Main category: q-bio.PE

TL;DR: 本文提出了一种高效算法，用于计算无向图上两个随机游走者的预期相遇时间，将计算复杂度从O(N⁶)降低到O(N⁴)，并理论上可进一步优化到O(N³log²N)。


<details>
  <summary>Details</summary>
Motivation: 计算两个随机游走者在无向图上的预期相遇时间需要解决包含C(N,2)个线性方程的系统，朴素方法需要O(N⁶)次操作，计算成本过高。虽然该系统具有类似Sylvester方程的良好结构，但存在对角吸收约束的障碍。

Method: 利用线性方程系统的特殊结构（近似Sylvester方程），提出了一种简单算法，通过标准密集线性代数实现O(N⁴)次操作和Θ(N²)空间。进一步利用对角修正的Cauchy结构，理论上可将复杂度优化到O(N³log²N)。该方法可推广到具有任意源的"懒惰"对游走的Poisson方程。

Result: 算法成功将计算所有C(N,2)个相遇时间的复杂度从O(N⁶)降低到O(N⁴)，空间需求为Θ(N²)。理论优化后可达O(N³log²N)复杂度。对于具有额外源的相同图，每个额外源的计算成本为O(N³)。

Conclusion: 提出的高效算法显著降低了随机游走者相遇时间的计算复杂度，并成功应用于进化动力学中，改进了固定概率和平均性状频率的计算算法。

Abstract: The expected meeting time of two random walkers on an undirected graph of size $N$, where at each time step one walker moves and the process stops when they collide, satisfies a system of $\binom{N}{2}$ linear equations. Naïvely, solving this system takes $O\left(N^{6}\right)$ operations. However, this system of linear equations has nice structure in that it is almost a Sylvester equation, with the obstruction being a diagonal absorption constraint. We give a simple algorithm for solving this system that exploits this structure, leading to $O\left(N^{4}\right)$ operations and $Θ\left(N^{2}\right)$ space for exact computation of all $\binom{N}{2}$ meeting times. While this practical method uses only standard dense linear algebra, it can be improved (in theory) to $O\left(N^{3}\log^{2}N\right)$ operations by exploiting the Cauchy structure of the diagonal correction. We generalize this result slightly to cover the Poisson equation for the absorbing "lazy" pair walk with an arbitrary source, which can be solved at the same cost, with $O\left(N^{3}\right)$ per additional source on the same graph. We conclude with applications to evolutionary dynamics, giving improved algorithms for calculating fixation probabilities and mean trait frequencies.

<details>
  <summary>Details</summary>

Method: 提出Dual Triangle Attention，将每个注意力头的查询-键子空间分成两个互补的三角掩码：一个关注过去和自身位置，另一个关注未来和自身位置。使用PyTorch的flex_attention实现为单个编译内核调用，除了标准多头注意力外没有额外参数。

Result: 在三个设置中评估：1）合成argmax位置探测任务：Dual Triangle Attention和因果注意力都能在没有显式位置嵌入的情况下学习位置信息，而标准双向注意力不能；2）自然语言掩码语言建模；3）蛋白质序列掩码语言建模。Dual Triangle Attention结合RoPE在上下文扩展性能上表现最佳，整体性能强劲。

Conclusion: Dual Triangle Attention是一种可行的双向Transformer注意力机制，无论是否使用位置嵌入都能有效工作，提供了双向上下文同时保持了因果掩码的隐式位置归纳偏置。

Abstract: Bidirectional transformers are the foundation of many sequence modeling tasks across natural, biological, and chemical language domains, but they are permutation-invariant without explicit positional embeddings. In contrast, unidirectional attention inherently encodes positional information through its triangular mask, enabling models to operate without positional embeddings altogether. Here, we introduce Dual Triangle Attention, a novel bidirectional attention mechanism that separates the query-key subspace of each attention head into two complementary triangular masks: one that attends to past-and-self positions and one that attends to future-and-self positions. This design provides bidirectional context while maintaining the causal mask's implicit positional inductive bias in both directions. Using PyTorch's flex_attention, Dual Triangle Attention is implemented as a single compiled kernel call with no additional parameters beyond standard multi-head attention. We evaluated Dual Triangle Attention across three settings: (1) a synthetic argmax position probe, (2) masked language modeling (MLM) on natural language, and (3) MLM on protein sequences. In the argmax task, both Dual Triangle Attention and causal attention learn positional information without explicit positional embeddings, whereas standard bidirectional attention cannot. In the MLM experiments, Dual Triangle Attention with Rotary Positional Embeddings (RoPE) achieved the best context extension performance and strong performance across the board. These findings suggest that Dual Triangle Attention is a viable attention mechanism for bidirectional transformers, with or without positional embeddings.
