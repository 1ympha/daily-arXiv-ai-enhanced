### [1] [Subspace Tensor Orthogonal Rotation Model (STORM) for Batch Alignment, Cell Type Deconvolution, and Gene Imputation in Spatial Transcriptomic Data](https://arxiv.org/abs/2603.22477)
*Sean Cottrell,Guo-Wei Wei,Longxiu Huang*

Main category: q-bio.QM

TL;DR: STORM提出了一种用于空间转录组学数据分析的新的子空间张量正交旋转模型，通过不规则张量分解技术整合多个切片数据，解决批次效应、混合细胞类型信号和基因表达缺失值问题。


<details>
  <summary>Details</summary>
Motivation: 空间转录组学数据分析面临三个主要挑战：批次效应处理、混合细胞类型信号处理以及测量不佳或缺失基因表达值的填补。现有方法在这些问题上仍有不足。

Method: 提出STORM模型，采用不规则张量分解技术，将多个空间维度不同的切片数据分解并整合到共享的潜在空间中。该方法考虑了物理模式或微环境层面的对齐，相比黑盒深度学习方法具有固有的可解释性。

Result: 数值实验表明，STORM在垂直和水平批次整合、细胞类型反卷积以及空间转录组学数据中未测量基因填补方面达到了最先进的性能。

Conclusion: STORM为空间转录组学数据分析提供了一个有效且可解释的框架，能够同时解决多个关键挑战，在多个任务上表现出色。

Abstract: Spatial transcriptomics data analysis integrates cellular transcriptional activity with spatial coordinates to identify spatial domains, infer cell-type dynamics, and characterize gene expression patterns within tissues. Despite recent advances, significant challenges remain, including the treatment of batch effects, the handling of mixed cell-type signals, and the imputation of poorly measured or missing gene expression. This work addresses these challenges by introducing a novel Subspace Tensor Orthogonal Rotation Model (STORM) that aligns multiple slices which vary in their spatial dimensions and geometry by considering them at the level of physical patterns or microenvironments. To this end, STORM presents an irregular tensor factorization technique for decomposing a collection of gene expression matrices and integrating them into a shared latent space for downstream analysis. In contrast to black-box deep learning approaches, the proposed model is inherently interpretable. Numerical experiments demonstrate state-of-the-art performance in vertical and horizontal batch integration, cell-type deconvolution, and unmeasured gene imputation for spatial transcriptomics data.

### [2] [Modelling SARS-CoV-2 epidemics via compartmental and cellular automaton SEIRS model with temporal immunity and vaccination](https://arxiv.org/abs/2603.22498)
*J. Ilnytskyi,T. Patsahan*

Main category: q-bio.PE

TL;DR: 该研究分析了包含未识别感染者、有限免疫时间和疫苗接种可能性的SEIRS流行病学模型，探讨了通过限制传播率、提高感染者识别隔离率以及疫苗接种来控制疫情动态的方法。


<details>
  <summary>Details</summary>
Motivation: 研究动机是分析COVID-19疫情的特征，包括大量未识别感染者、有限免疫时间和疫苗接种可能性，并探索通过多种控制措施来管理疫情动态。

Method: 方法包括：1）建立包含未识别感染者、有限免疫时间和疫苗接种的SEIRS隔室模型；2）分析模型的无病和流行稳定状态；3）研究基本再生数与隔离和疫苗接种措施的关系；4）数值计算疫情首次峰值位置和高度并拟合为简单代数形式；5）使用异步元胞自动机算法实现基于格点的模型，研究社交距离对疫情传播的影响。

Result: 研究发现：1）确定了疾病无病和流行的稳定状态；2）分析了基本再生数与隔离和疫苗接种措施的平衡关系；3）获得了疫情首次峰值的位置和高度，并拟合为易于使用的代数形式；4）通过元胞自动机模拟发现，通过改变邻域大小可以研究社交距离效应；5）尝试匹配隔离和疫苗接种的效果。

Conclusion: 该研究为COVID-19疫情控制提供了综合建模框架，表明通过结合传播率限制、感染者识别隔离和疫苗接种等多种措施可以有效控制疫情，同时通过元胞自动机方法能够研究社交距离等空间因素对疫情传播的影响。

Abstract: We consider the SEIRS epidemiology model with such features of the COVID-19 outbreak as: abundance of unidentified infected individuals, limited time of immunity and a possibility of vaccination. The control of the pandemic dynamics is possible by restricting the transmission rate, increasing identification and isolation rate of infected individuals, and via vaccination. For the compartmental version of this model, we found stable disease-free and endemic stationary states. The basic reproductive number is analysed with respect to balancing quarantine and vaccination measures. The positions and heights of the first peak of outbreak are obtained numerically and fitted to simple in usage algebraic forms. Lattice-based realization of this model is studied by means of the asynchronous cellular automaton algorithm. This permitted to study the effect of social distancing by varying the neighbourhood size of the model. The attempt is made to match the quarantine and vaccination effects.

### [3] [Balancing training load, rest and musculoskeletal injury risk: a mathematical modelling study in Thoroughbred racehorses](https://arxiv.org/abs/2603.22680)
*Md Nurul Anwar,Michael Pan,Ashleigh V. Morrice-West,Fatemeh Malekipour,Peter Pivonka,Jennifer A. Flegg,R Chris Whitton,Peta L. Hitchens*

Main category: q-bio.PE

TL;DR: 该研究使用数学模型分析不同训练方案对纯血赛马骨骼损伤风险的影响，发现低强度训练结合定期休息能降低骨骼损伤风险


<details>
  <summary>Details</summary>
Motivation: 肌肉骨骼损伤是纯血赛马死亡和提前退役的主要原因，与训练实践密切相关。了解训练实践如何影响骨骼损伤积累对于改善马匹福利和比赛结果至关重要

Method: 基于现有骨骼适应和损伤数学模型，构建了13种代表性训练方案（包括渐进式、比赛准备、休息方案等），分析训练量、休息频率和持续时间对骨骼损伤风险的影响

Result: 低强度训练方案在实现足够骨骼适应的同时减少了骨骼损伤积累；每年至少2次休息和/或至少6周休息期能显著降低骨骼损伤风险

Conclusion: 研究提供了骨骼对训练反应的数学模型，证明训练方案设计可以平衡骨骼适应和骨骼健康，为设计更安全的赛马训练方案提供指导

Abstract: Musculoskeletal injuries (MSI) in Thoroughbred racehorses are a leading cause of death and premature retirement in racehorses and are heavily influenced by training practices. Greater distances of high-speed galloping accumulated during racing campaigns are associated with MSI. Bone injury is the most common MSI, and understanding how training practices influence bone damage accumulation is critical for improving both horse welfare and racing outcomes. This study builds on an existing mathematical model of bone adaptation and damage to investigate the impact of different training programs on bone injury risk. Several training programs (three progressive, four race-fit, six rest programs and two with rest replaced by low-intensity training) were constructed to reflect representative practices undertaken by professional trainers in Victoria, Australia. Training programs varied in training volume, rest frequency and program duration. Lower volume training programs that included high-speed training, achieved sufficient bone adaptation with less accumulation of bone damage, and subsequently lower risk of bone failure. In addition, incorporating more frequent rests (at least 2 per year) and/or longer rest periods (at least 6 weeks) reduced bone damage due to the extended opportunity to remove and repair bone damage. These results provide an in-silico mathematical model of the bone response to training, demonstrating the effects of training programs on bone adaptation, damage formation and repair. The findings can guide the design of training programs that balance both bone adaptation and bone health throughout horses racing career.
