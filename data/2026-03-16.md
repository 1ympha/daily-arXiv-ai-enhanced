### [1] [SHREC: A Spectral Embedding-Based Approach for Ab-Initio Reconstruction of Helical Molecules](https://arxiv.org/abs/2603.12307)
*Guy Shapira,Yoel Shkolnisky*

Main category: q-bio.QM

TL;DR: SHREC算法无需先验螺旋对称参数，直接从冷冻电镜图像恢复螺旋片段的投影角度，实现更稳健的螺旋结构重建。

Motivation: 冷冻电镜重建螺旋组装体面临独特挑战，传统方法需要准确的初始螺旋对称参数估计，这通常通过试错或先验知识获得，可能导致错误重建，限制了从头螺旋重建的可靠性。

Method: SHREC算法利用螺旋片段投影形成一维流形的洞察，使用谱嵌入技术直接从二维冷冻电镜图像恢复螺旋片段的投影角度，无需螺旋对称参数的先验知识。

Result: 在公开数据集上的实验验证表明，SHREC实现了高分辨率重建，同时准确恢复螺旋参数，仅需要样本的轴向对称群知识。

Conclusion: 通过消除对初始对称估计的需求，SHREC为冷冻电镜中螺旋结构的确定提供了更稳健和自动化的途径。

Abstract: Cryo-electron microscopy (cryo-EM) has emerged as a powerful technique for determining the three-dimensional structures of biological molecules at near-atomic resolution. However, reconstructing helical assemblies presents unique challenges due to their inherent symmetry and the need to determine unknown helical symmetry parameters. Traditional approaches require an accurate initial estimation of these parameters, which is often obtained through trial and error or prior knowledge. These requirements can lead to incorrect reconstructions, limiting the reliability of ab initio helical reconstruction.
  In this work, we present SHREC (Spectral Helical REConstruction), an algorithm that directly recovers the projection angles of helical segments from their two-dimensional cryo-EM images, without requiring prior knowledge of helical symmetry parameters. Our approach leverages the insight that projections of helical segments form a one-dimensional manifold, which can be recovered using spectral embedding techniques. Experimental validation on publicly available datasets demonstrates that SHREC achieves high resolution reconstructions while accurately recovering helical parameters, requiring only knowledge of the specimen's axial symmetry group. By eliminating the need for initial symmetry estimates, SHREC offers a more robust and automated pathway for determining helical structures in cryo-EM.
