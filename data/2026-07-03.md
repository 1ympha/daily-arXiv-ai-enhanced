### [1] [Affinage: genome-scale mechanistic gene annotation from the published literature](https://arxiv.org/abs/2607.02217)
*Matteo Di Bernardo,Iain M. Cheeseman*

Main category: q-bio.GN

TL;DR: Affinage是一个LLM管道，能够从原始文献中自动提取直接实验证据，为19,293个人类蛋白质编码基因生成可复用的结构化功能注释，在99.1%的对比中优于UniProt数据库，并识别出10%尚未被机制性表征的蛋白质组。


<details>
  <summary>Details</summary>
Motivation: 人类蛋白质组中大部分基因的功能知识分散在大量文献中，或尚未被充分确立，而现有的数据库更新滞后。需要一种可扩展、可复用的方法来自动化地从文献中提取和综合基因功能信息。

Method: 构建了一个LLM管道，包含两个核心阶段：1) 阅读阶段——由生物学家设计的阅读策略，仅提取直接实验证据；2) 综合阶段——基于这些证据进行机理推理。对每个基因只执行一次检索和推理，结果存储为结构化注释，可跨用户复用。

Result: Affinage成功注释了19,293个人类蛋白质编码基因。对于UniProt数据库中功能为空或仅含简要描述的基因提供了机理注释，在99.1%的头对头比较中击败了UniProt（由跨家族LLM评判器评估）。同时识别出约10%仍未被机理性表征的蛋白质组。

Conclusion: Affinage提供了一个持续更新、基于文献的基因功能普查，展示了领域专家如何将专业知识编码到可扩展的LLM管道中，以改进指导生物学假设和实验的公开数据。

Abstract: Understanding the mechanistic function of a gene is a critical starting point for biology. However, for much of the human proteome that knowledge is scattered across thousands of primary papers or remains poorly established, while the curated databases biologists rely on can lag years behind recent literature. Large language models can now read and synthesize that literature on demand, but doing so faithfully for many genes is an expensive, non-reproducible retrieval session that does not scale across users. Here, we present Affinage, an LLM pipeline that performs this retrieval and mechanistic reasoning once per gene--from the primary literature alone--and stores the result as a reusable, structured annotation. A biologist-designed reading pass extracts only direct experimental evidence, and a synthesis pass reasons over those findings alone. Applied across the genome, Affinage annotates 19,293 human protein-coding genes. This analysis provides mechanism for thousands of genes whose UniProt function is empty or a stub, beating the curated reference on 99.1% of head-to-head genes as scored by a cross-family LLM judge. Affinage also delineates the 10% of the proteome that remains mechanistically uncharacterized and will serve as a continuously-updated, literature-grounded census of gene function. All records are released openly at https://affinage.wi.mit.edu . More broadly, Affinage serves as an example of how domain experts can encode their expertise into scalable LLM pipelines to improve the publicly available data that guides biological hypotheses and experimentation.

### [2] [Structured Gaussian Processes for Uncertainty-Aware Classification of High-Dimensional, Small-Sampled Omics Data](https://arxiv.org/abs/2607.02103)
*Yue Zhang,Nandini Amit Gadhia,Georgios Karagiannis,Michalis Smyrnakis*

Main category: q-bio.QM

TL;DR: 提出了一种结构化高斯过程分类框架，通过将生物学通路图编码到核函数中，结合已知交互网络与丰度特征，提升了高维小样本组学数据的分类性能，并有效处理了类别不平衡问题。


<details>
  <summary>Details</summary>
Motivation: 高维小样本组学数据中非线性交互占主导、类别不平衡，传统核方法依赖特征丰度但未能利用已知的生物系统交互网络结构。

Method: 提出结构化高斯过程分类框架，将图编码的生物通路直接融入核函数构造，通过沿已知交互网络传播信息并与丰度特征结合，同时采用数据重采样、阈值校准等策略处理类别不平衡。

Result: 在三个公开肠道/粪便微生物组数据集上，混合方法优于非结构化基线，与已有基准方法性能相当，且提供校准后的预测不确定性。

Conclusion: 将生物学通路结构信息融入高斯过程核函数能够有效提升组学分类性能，概率框架自然提供可靠的不确定性估计，有助于区分置信预测与模糊样本。

Abstract: Classifying heterogeneous omics data remains a fundamental challenge in computational biology, particularly in high-dimensional, small-sample settings where nonlinear interactions dominate and class imbalance further complicates reliable prediction of minority phenotypes. While traditional kernel methods rely on feature abundance, they fail to leverage the known interaction landscapes of biological systems.
  In this work, we propose a structured Gaussian process classification framework that integrates graph-encoded biological pathways directly into the kernel construction. By propagating information along known interaction networks and combining this with abundance-derived features, the resulting classifier captures both quantitative measurements and topological context.
  We benchmark our proposed methodology on three publicly available gut and fecal microbiome datasets. To address severe class imbalance, we evaluate complementary strategies, including data-level resampling, threshold calibration, and confusion-matrix-based adjustments, and report minority-class performance alongside accuracy. The hybrid approach yields a performance gain over unstructured baselines and matches the performance of established benchmarks for similar datasets. Furthermore, the probabilistic nature of the framework naturally provides calibrated predictive uncertainty, enabling robust differentiation between confident predictions and ambiguous samples.

</details>
