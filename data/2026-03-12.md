### [1] [SDSR: A Spectral Divide-and-Conquer Approach for Species Tree Reconstruction](https://arxiv.org/abs/2603.10215)
*Ortal Reshef,Ofer Glassman,Or Zuk,Yariv Aizenbud,Boaz Nadler,Ariel Jaffe*

Main category: q-bio.PE

TL;DR: SDSR是一种基于谱图理论的可扩展分治方法，用于物种树重建，通过递归分区和合并子树来显著提高计算效率，同时保持重建精度。


<details>
  <summary>Details</summary>
Motivation: 解决系统发育学中的两个关键挑战：1）单个基因进化史与物种进化史之间的不一致性；2）涉及数千个物种的当代研究的计算复杂性。

Method: SDSR采用基于谱图理论的可扩展分治方法：递归将物种划分为子集直到大小低于阈值，使用用户选择的物种树算法重建这些子集的树，然后将这些子树合并形成完整树。

Result: 理论分析表明SDSR在多物种溯祖模型下具有恢复保证，运行时分析显示与完整数据应用相同算法相比可节省大量时间。实证评估显示，与CA-ML或ASTRAL等方法结合时，运行时间最多可提高10倍，同时保持与完整数据应用这些方法相当的树重建精度。

Conclusion: SDSR是一种高效且准确的物种树重建方法，通过分治策略显著提高了计算效率，同时保持了重建精度，特别适用于大规模系统发育分析。

Abstract: Recovering a tree that represents the evolutionary history of a group of species is a key task in phylogenetics. Performing this task using sequence data from multiple genetic markers poses two key challenges. The first is the discordance between the evolutionary history of individual genes and that of the species. The second challenge is computational, as contemporary studies involve thousands of species. Here we present SDSR, a scalable divide-and-conquer approach for species tree reconstruction based on spectral graph theory. The algorithm recursively partitions the species into subsets until their sizes are below a given threshold. The trees of these subsets are reconstructed by a user-chosen species tree algorithm. Finally, these subtrees are merged to form the full tree. On the theoretical front, we derive recovery guarantees for SDSR, under the multispecies coalescent (MSC) model. We also perform a runtime complexity analysis. We show that SDSR, when combined with a species tree reconstruction algorithm as a subroutine, yields substantial runtime savings as compared to applying the same algorithm on the full data. Empirically, we evaluate SDSR on synthetic benchmark datasets with incomplete lineage sorting and horizontal gene transfer. In accordance with our theoretical analysis, the simulations show that combining SDSR with common species tree methods, such as CA-ML or ASTRAL, yields up to 10-fold faster runtimes. In addition, SDSR achieves a comparable tree reconstruction accuracy to that obtained by applying these methods on the full data.

### [2] [Macroscopic Dominance from Microscopic Extremes: Symmetry Breaking in Spatial Competition](https://arxiv.org/abs/2603.10911)
*Stuti Guha,Shawn D. Ryan,Bhargav R. Karamched*

Main category: q-bio.PE

TL;DR: 该研究探讨了竞争种群如何将空间优势转化为宏观主导地位，提出了一个随机资源竞争模型，将瞬时发现阶段与垄断阶段解耦，揭示了线性空间劣势需要指数级更大种群来克服，并指出非互惠交互偏差对于稳定主导地位的必要性。


<details>
  <summary>Details</summary>
Motivation: 研究动机是理解竞争种群如何将空间优势转化为宏观主导地位，探索在资源竞争中初始对称性破缺的机制以及稳定主导地位所需的条件。

Method: 引入了一个随机资源竞争模型，将瞬时发现阶段与垄断阶段解耦。通过分析首次通过时间的极值统计来研究初始对称性破缺，并探讨非互惠交互偏差对系统稳定性的影响。

Result: 研究发现：1）初始对称性破缺由首次通过时间的极值统计控制，线性空间劣势需要指数级更大的种群来克服；2）瞬时优势无法稳定主导地位；3）非互惠交互偏差对于抑制局部波动并将系统驱动到稳健吸收态是严格必要的。

Conclusion: 空间优势转化为宏观主导地位需要两个关键阶段：初始对称性破缺（由极值统计控制）和稳定化（需要非互惠交互偏差）。仅靠空间优势或瞬时发现优势不足以建立稳定的主导地位，必须存在非互惠机制来维持垄断状态。

Abstract: How do competing populations convert a spatial advantage into macroscopic dominance? We introduce a stochastic model for resource competition that decouples the transient discovery phase from monopolization. Initial symmetry breaking is governed by extreme value statistics of first-passage times: a linear spatial disadvantage requires an exponentially larger population to overcome. However, transient superiority cannot stabilize dominance. A non-reciprocal interaction bias is strictly necessary to arrest local fluctuations and drive the system into a robust absorbing state.
