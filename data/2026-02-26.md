### [1] [From female choice to social structure: Modeling harem formation in camelids](https://arxiv.org/abs/2602.22139)
*Tomás Ignacio González,Guillermo Abramson,María Fabiana Laguna*

Main category: q-bio.PE

TL;DR: 研究开发了一个基于个体的随机模型，模拟雌性骆驼科动物在不同环境条件下为最大化个体适应度而在家族群间转移的行为，发现个体行为策略能塑造群体层面的涌现特性。


<details>
  <summary>Details</summary>
Motivation: 草食性野生动物需要在能量/营养摄入与捕食风险之间进行权衡优化，这导致了饮食、栖息地选择和行为等进化特征的产生。骆驼科动物中普遍存在的一夫多妻制行为策略同时影响交配和觅食活动，这种群体行为具有多个相互作用的维度，形成了有趣的生态和进化复杂性。

Method: 开发了一个基于个体的随机模型，模拟雌性骆驼科动物根据环境条件在不同家族群之间转移，以最大化个体适应度的行为策略。

Result: 结果表明，个体雌性的行为策略本身能够塑造涌现的种群层面特性，包括群体大小和适应度分布。这些特性受到种群密度、性别比例和系统异质性等因素的非加性调节。

Conclusion: 个体层面的行为策略对种群层面的生态特性具有重要影响，这种影响受到多种环境因素的非线性调节，揭示了行为生态学中微观与宏观层面之间的复杂相互作用。

Abstract: Herbivorous wild species constantly strive to optimize the trade-off between energy and nutrient intake and predation risk during foraging. This has led to the selection of several evolutionary traits -- such as diet, habitat selection, and behavior -- which are simultaneously shaped by the spatio-temporal variability of the habitat. Among camelid species, polygyny is a prevalent behavioral strategy that encompasses both mating and foraging activities. This group-level behavior has multiple interacting dimensions, contributing to an interesting ecological and evolutionary complexity. We developed an individual-based stochastic model in which camelid females transition between different familial groups in response to their environmental conditions, aiming to maximize individual fitness. Our results indicate that the behavioral strategy of individual females can shape, by itself, emergent population-level properties, including group size and fitness distribution. Furthermore, these properties are modulated, in a non-additive manner, by other factors such as population density, sex ratio and system heterogeneity.

### [2] [BEDCrypt: Privacy-preserving interval analytics with homomorphic encryption](https://arxiv.org/abs/2602.21994)
*Kimon Antonios Provatas,Ilias Georgakopoulos-Soares*

Main category: q-bio.GN

TL;DR: BEDCrypt是一个基于同态加密的隐私保护基因组区间分析系统，在诚实但好奇的服务器设置中保护基因组数据和查询位点的隐私。


<details>
  <summary>Details</summary>
Motivation: 基因组数据及其衍生的区间数据集可能包含敏感信息，分析过程本身也可能暴露分析者的意图。随着基因组工作负载越来越多地外包给第三方基础设施，需要能够同时保护数据和查询位点的隐私保护技术。

Method: 基于同态加密的隐私保护系统，在诚实但好奇的服务器设置中运行。服务器仅操作加密数据并返回加密答案，客户端在本地解密，实现覆盖度摘要、区间交集、邻近性（窗口式）查询和集合相似性统计等核心功能。

Result: 开发了BEDCrypt系统，能够在服务器不接触明文区间或查询基因组位置的情况下，实现隐私保护的基因组区间分析功能。

Conclusion: BEDCrypt为基因组区间分析提供了一种有效的隐私保护解决方案，在数据外包到第三方基础设施时保护数据隐私和查询意图。

Abstract: Motivation. Genomic data and derived interval datasets can carry sensitive information, and the analysis itself can reveal an analyst's intent. As genomic workloads are increasingly outsourced to third-party infrastructure, there is a need for privacy-preserving technologies that protect both the data and the queried loci. Results. We present BEDCrypt, a privacy-preserving system for genomic interval analytics based on homomorphic encryption in an honest-but-curious server setting. The server operates only on encrypted data and returns encrypted answers that the client decrypts locally, enabling core functionalities such as coverage summaries, interval intersections, proximity (window-style) queries, and set-similarity statistics, without revealing plaintext intervals or query genomic locations to the server.

### [3] [An information-based model selection criterion for data-driven model discovery](https://arxiv.org/abs/2602.21393)
*Michael C Chung,Alen Zacharia,Juan Guan*

Main category: q-bio.QM

TL;DR: 提出一种自动生成候选模型并使用新型样本长度缩放对数信息准则(SLIC)选择最优模型的稀疏回归算法，显著优于现有信息准则，能有效从实验数据中发现可解释模型。


<details>
  <summary>Details</summary>
Motivation: 传统数据驱动模型发现算法需要大量人工调参、容易过拟合、结果对初始化和训练过程敏感，缺乏自动选择最优模型的有效方法。

Method: 开发了一种稀疏回归算法，自动自适应生成候选模型，并使用新型样本长度缩放对数信息准则(SLIC)从候选模型中识别最佳模型。

Result: SLIC在从非线性常微分方程和偏微分方程数据中提取正确模型方面显著优于其他流行信息准则，并能从流体动力学和纳米技术实验数据中发现可解释模型，生成新的可测试预测。

Conclusion: 提出的SLIC算法能够自动、自适应地发现最优稀疏模型，解决了传统方法需要人工调参和容易过拟合的问题，在多个领域展现出强大的模型发现能力。

Abstract: Data-driven model discovery (DDMD) algorithms are powerful tools for extracting interpretable symbolic models from data. However, identifying the model that best balances goodness-of-fit and sparsity is often a laborious process requiring user fine-tuning, is prone to overfitting, and results may significantly vary depending on model initialization and specific training procedure. Here, we present a sparse regression algorithm that automatically and adaptively generates candidate models, and uses a novel sample-length-scaling logarithmic information criterion (SLIC) to identify the best model from these candidates. We demonstrate that SLIC greatly outperforms other popular information criteria in extracting the correct model from the data of several nonlinear ordinary and partial differential equations. Then, we demonstrate SLIC's ability to discover interpretable models from experimental datasets in fluid dynamics and nanotechnology that generate new testable predictions.

</details>
